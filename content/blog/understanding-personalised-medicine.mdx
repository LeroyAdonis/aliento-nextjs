## One Size Does Not Fit All

Two people can have the same diagnosis and respond completely differently to the same medication. Your genes, your environment, your lifestyle — they all shape how your body works. Personalised medicine recognises this instead of ignoring it.

### What It Actually Means

Personalised medicine (also called precision medicine) tailors healthcare decisions to *you* — your genetic makeup, your family history, your daily habits, even the environment you live in. It replaces guesswork with precision.

Think of it this way: traditional medicine hands everyone the same map. Personalised medicine gives you a GPS calibrated to your exact location.

### Why It Matters for South Africans

South Africa's population is incredibly diverse — genetically, culturally, and environmentally. A treatment plan designed around European clinical trials may not work the same way for you. Personalised medicine accounts for this diversity.

**The stats are clear:** Up to 30% of medications don't work as intended because of genetic differences between patients. That's billions of rands wasted and, more importantly, people not getting better.

### The Real Benefits

- **More accurate diagnoses** through genetic testing and advanced diagnostics that look deeper than symptoms
- **Targeted treatments** with fewer side effects — because the medication is chosen for your biology
- **Preventive care** that's specific to your risk factors, not generic population advice
- **Better outcomes** through monitoring that's tailored to how your body actually responds

### It's Not Just About Your DNA

Genetics is one piece of the puzzle. Personalised medicine also considers:

- **Family medical history** — what runs in your family matters enormously
- **Your environment** — where you live, what you're exposed to daily
- **Lifestyle factors** — diet, exercise, stress, sleep patterns
- **Your values** — because the best treatment is one you'll actually follow
