## The Short Answer

**When in doubt, get checked out.** But here's a more detailed guide to help you decide.

### Always Go Immediately For:

- Chest pain or difficulty breathing
- Sudden severe headache
- Weakness on one side of the face or body
- Severe bleeding that won't stop
- Confusion or altered consciousness

### Schedule an Appointment Within 24 Hours For:

- Fever above 38.5°C that won't come down
- Persistent pain that's getting worse
- Unexplained weight loss
- Changes in moles or skin lesions
- Symptoms that don't improve after a few days

### Routine Check-Ups (Don't Skip These!)

Even when you feel perfectly fine:

- **Every 1-2 years:** General health screening
- **Annually:** Blood pressure, cholesterol, blood sugar
- **Every 3-5 years:** Full blood work panel
- **As recommended:** Age-appropriate screenings (mammograms, colonoscopies, etc.)

### Prevention Is Better Than Cure

The best time to see a doctor is **before** you feel sick. Regular check-ups can catch issues early when they're easier and less expensive to treat.
